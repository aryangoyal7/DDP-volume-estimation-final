========================================================================
  DEMO NARRATION SCRIPT — Text-Based Selection & Volume Estimation
  Read this aloud while recording. ~2 minutes. Pause where marked [PAUSE].
========================================================================

------------------------------------------------------------------------
0. BEFORE YOU HIT RECORD (checklist — do not read aloud)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  - App is running at  http://localhost:7862
  - Browser zoomed to a comfortable level, window maximized
  - Hard refresh once (Ctrl+Shift+R) so the latest UI loads
  - Have a top-down photo of a pile ready to drag in
  - Default mode is already "Prompted segmentation" — good

------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. INTRO  (camera on the full page)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
"Hi. In this demo I'll show our Volume Estimator, which measures the
 volume of an object from a single top-down photo.

 What's new here is text-based selection — instead of clicking or drawing
 a box, I just type in plain English what I want to measure, and the app
 finds it, outlines it, and estimates only that region."

[PAUSE — let the page sit on screen for a second]

------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. THE MODELS  (optional — keep if the audience is technical)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
"Under the hood it uses two models: Depth Anything V2 for metric depth,
 which gave us the most accurate results, and LangSAM — that's Grounding
 DINO plus SAM 2.1 — for the text-driven segmentation."

------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. UPLOAD  (Step 1 panel)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
"First, Step 1 — I upload a top-down photo of the pile inside the
 calibration frame."

[ACTION: drag the image into the upload box, wait for it to appear]

------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. TEXT SELECTION  (Step 2 panel — this is the key moment)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
"Now the important part — Step 2, 'What do you want to measure?'

 The selection method is set to 'Prompted segmentation', and here in the
 text box I simply describe the object."

[ACTION: click the prompt box, clear it, type:  waste pile ]

"I'll type 'waste pile'. That's it — no clicking on the image, no drawing.
 You could just as easily type 'cardboard boxes' or 'sand heap' and it
 would target that instead."

------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. CALIBRATION  (Step 3 panel)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
"Step 3 lets me pick a calibration preset. I'll leave it on the global
 median, which matches our reference setup."

------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. RUN  (the Estimate button)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
"And now I press 'Estimate volume'."

[ACTION: click Estimate. While it runs:]

"It's running depth estimation and segmentation now — this takes a few
 seconds on the GPU."

[PAUSE — wait for results to render]

------------------------------------------------------------------------
7. RESULTS  (point at each part)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
"And here are the results.

 At the top we get the calibrated volume in litres and the estimated mass
 in kilograms.

 Below that: the raw volume, the mean and maximum height of the pile, and
 the coverage — how much of the frame the object filled.

 There's also a depth-sanity badge confirming the centre reads closer than
 the edges, which tells us the depth map is sensible."

[ACTION: switch the visualization tabs on the right]

"On the right I can switch between three views:
   - the Height heatmap, showing where the pile is tallest,
   - the Depth map,
   - and the Mask, which shows exactly what my text prompt selected."

[ACTION: open the 'Mask' tab so viewers see the outlined region]

"Notice the mask matches only the pile I described — that's the
 text-based selection working."

------------------------------------------------------------------------
8. SECOND PROMPT  (optional — shows flexibility)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
"To show how flexible this is, let me change the prompt to something else
 in the same image and re-run."

[ACTION: type a different object, e.g.  cardboard boxes , press Estimate]

"Same photo, different target — selected purely from the text."

------------------------------------------------------------------------
9. CLOSE
------------------------------------------------------------------------
"So that's the workflow: upload a photo, type what to measure, and get a
 calibrated volume and mass — with no manual annotation. Thanks for
 watching."

  END
